## 8. Funding Scenarios

### Scenario A: Bootstrap (No Funding)

```
Year 1: Solo + 1 contractor → $100K ARR → breakeven
Year 2: Hire 3 employees → $731K ARR → profitable
Year 3: Scale to 15 people → $3.56M ARR → highly profitable

Pros: No dilution, full control
Cons: Slower growth, limited marketing
```

### Scenario B: Seed Round ($500K)

```
Year 1: Hire 3 employees + marketing → $200K ARR
Year 2: Scale to 10 people → $1.5M ARR
Year 3: Scale to 20 people → $5M ARR

Use of funds:
  60% Engineering ($300K) → Complete platform, new agents
  20% GTM ($100K) → Marketing, sales, conferences
  10% Infrastructure ($50K) → Managed hosting, APIs
  10% Operations ($50K) → Legal, admin, tools

Pros: Faster growth, bigger market capture
Cons: 15-20% dilution
```

### Scenario C: Series A ($2-3M)

```
Year 1: Full team of 8 → $500K ARR
Year 2: Scale to 20 → $3M ARR
Year 3: Scale to 40 → $10M ARR

Use of funds:
  50% Engineering ($1.25M) → Platform + marketplace
  25% Sales & Marketing ($625K) → Enterprise sales, content
  15% Infrastructure ($375K) → Global hosting, redundancy
  10% Operations ($250K) → Team, legal, admin

Pros: Market leader, fast execution
Cons: 20-25% dilution, pressure to scale
```
